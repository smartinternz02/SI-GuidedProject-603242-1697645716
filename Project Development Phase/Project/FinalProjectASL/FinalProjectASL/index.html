American Sign Language (ASL) is a vibrant and expressive visual language used by the Deaf and Hard of Hearing community in the United States and many parts of Canada. ASL is a fully developed language with its own grammar and syntax, relying on a rich vocabulary of handshapes, movements, facial expressions, and body language to convey meaning. It is a powerful and vital means of communication for millions of individuals.
